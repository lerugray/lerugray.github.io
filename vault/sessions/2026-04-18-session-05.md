## Addendum — late-session extension (same 2026-04-18 sitting)

After the main session-05 body was committed as Phase 23, Ray confirmed the live output looked right and signaled capacity for more work. Three further phases landed the same evening.

### Phase 24 — retrofit examination + essay vault draft

Two candidate surgical upgrades flagged earlier in the session (`/writing` index → `.entries` pattern; CSL umbrella credential strip → `.infra-meta` styling) were examined closely. **Both were rejected as premature abstraction**, not shipped:

- `/writing` index has title + metadata semantics (date + reading time), not the anchor's title + description. Swapping would either drop the metadata (information loss) or invent a metadata-instead-of-desc variant class for a two-entry page. Not earned.
- CSL's strip uses `border-y` hairlines around three unlabeled achievements. `.infra-meta` is unbordered flex with `.k` / `.v` key-value pairs. The achievements are not key-value, and the borders are more distinctive than the primitive. Forced retrofit would strip borders and invent fake key-value wrappers. Not earned.

Decision record updated with the findings and a sharpened rule: *"a page earns anchor-vocabulary retrofit only when (a) the semantic fit is exact and (b) the existing treatment is less distinctive or less reusable than the anchor primitive. If either fails, leave the page alone."* Grounded in two worked examples.

Boolean Gates essay drafted in `vault/boolean-gates-essay-draft.md`. Shape B (claims-first) per the earlier alignment. ~1600 words, paired with the Hammerstein essay as its architecture-layer companion. Voice calibrated against the Hammerstein piece: short declarative sentences, first-person practice note, admits trade-offs, no SaaS language. Three title candidates proposed; recommended (1) "Boolean Gates, Not Prompts."

### Phase 25 — Boolean Gates essay shipped

Stop-slop skill invoked on the vault draft. ### Phase 26 — post-deploy discoverability fix

Ray spot-checked the live site and flagged that the Boolean Gates essay was not reachable from the sidebar nav or the landing page — only from direct URL or the cross-links on `/generalstaff` and `/raybrain`.

Root cause: when Phase 22 rewired `navigation.ts` into the grouped structure, the Writing group was hardcoded with a single item (Hammerstein). That was correct at Phase 22 (only one essay existed). Phase 25 shipped Boolean Gates without updating the nav data. The landing page similarly had no Writing section — "Start here" featured the Hammerstein essay as the single editorial entry point, and there was no discovery path to a second essay.

Fix applied:

- `src/data/navigation.ts` — Writing group now lists both essays. Future essays added to `src/content/writing/` will need to be added here as well (content-collection loader handles `/writing` index rendering automatically, but sidebar nav is hand-maintained).
- `src/pages/index.astro` — new Writing section inserted between "Start here" and Businesses, using the anchor's `.entries` pattern (section-head "Writing" with count "02 ——", both essays listed with pitch descriptions). Hammerstein appears in both "Start here" (featured) and the Writing index list; the duplication matches a newspaper front-page / featured-plus-index pattern and was judged acceptable for the discovery gain.

Landing section order is now: Hero · Bio · Start here · **Writing** · Businesses · Infrastructure · Projects · Music.
