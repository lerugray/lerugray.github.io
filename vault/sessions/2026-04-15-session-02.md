## Design proposal (approved in one pass)

Ray asked for a structured proposal covering typography, palette, spacing, and component patterns — not code. Used the `ui-ux-pro-max` skill for reference context and synthesized the proposal against the content already in the vault, the voice in the bio and essay, and the historical-wargame signature. Three options for typography, two directions for palette, two routing choices. Ray approved all leans: **Option A (IBM Plex) + Ink on Parchment + multi-page routing + §4 component patterns.** Locked as [`../decisions/2026-04-15-design-system-v1.md`](../decisions/2026-04-15-design-system-v1.md).

Hammerstein check: this is the right shape of work. Ray had explicitly said "don't build until I approve the direction." The temptation was to jump to `npm run dev` and start prototyping. The clever-lazy move was to write the proposal, surface the three most consequential trade-offs (serif vs sans body, warm-dark vs cool-graphite, multi-page vs single-page), and let Ray's judgment steer before a single file changed in `src/`.
