## One-liner

A practitioner's guide to designing historical wargames, written by a designer of roughly thirty published wargames — covering the craft, the process, the tradeoffs, and the editorial sensibility that separates serious historical wargaming from cinematic war games.

## What it is

A book in roughly 23 chapters. Free to read online via a GitHub Pages reader, and also headed for KDP distribution. Authored by Ray Weiss — designer of ~30 published board wargames, CSR-Award-nominated, published by Multi-Man Publishing (MMP) — so the authority is real and the examples come from actual shipped games, not theory.

## What makes it distinctive

- **Written by a practicing designer**, not a games journalist. Chapter examples come from real published games and the design decisions behind them.
- **Contemporary scope.** It reflects what the wargame design conversation actually looks like now — operational depth, solo-play design, print-on-demand economics, AI-assisted prototyping, the relationship between historical accuracy and playability — not a mid-20th-century manual reprinted.
- **Honest about tradeoffs.** Aligns with Ray's broader signature of structural truth over marketing polish. A "how to design a wargame" book from someone who has actually shipped thirty of them is a different object from a general game-design book that treats wargaming as a subgenre.

## Audience

Aspiring wargame designers, serious wargame hobbyists who want to understand design decisions in games they already play, indie game developers curious about the wargame design tradition, historical game designers crossing over from other genres.
