## Why this subject matters

The Franco-Prussian War is one of the most consequential wars most people have never played a game about. It produced the modern German state, ended France's continental dominance, set up the conditions for the First World War, and rewrote the textbook on how armies should be organized — the Prussian general-staff system was studied and copied by nearly every military in the world afterward. It is exactly the kind of under-covered historical subject Ray's board wargame catalog has been mining for years, and DOAE is the digital version of that same editorial eye.

## Aesthetic fit

DOAE is terminal-first, meaning it lives in the same visual and attentional space as Veridian Contraption and Auftragstaktik. Ray's work tends toward interfaces that respect the player's attention and let systems be the star. If the wargame designer tradition is "serious subject matter, honest mechanics, no cinematic gloss," DOAE is a digital extension of it.

## Audience

Historical wargame hobbyists, 19th-century military history enthusiasts, terminal-game / indie-sim players whose tastes overlap with wargame nerds. Same audience that buys Ray's print-on-demand board wargames, with a probable overlap from the terminal-game crowd that already reads about Veridian Contraption.

## Relation to the rest of the portfolio

DOAE sits squarely inside the Conflict Simulations LLC publishing umbrella on its digital side, alongside Veridian Contraption, Devforge, and Prigozhin's March of Justice. It is also the game that most directly carries Ray's board wargame editorial sensibility into the digital catalog — a specific historical period handled seriously and at the right scale for a single-player terminal wargame.
